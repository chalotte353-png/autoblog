Eagles Land Potential Franchise Tight End in Eli Stowers

The Philadelphia Eagles continued to build around their offensive core on the second day of the 2026 NFL Draft, selecting Vanderbilt tight end Eli Stowers with one of their second-round picks. The selection signals the organization's commitment to upgrading the tight end position with a player who scouts have described as one of the most complete tight end prospects to come out of the SEC in recent memory.

Stowers, who spent his college career at Vanderbilt, emerged as one of the most closely watched tight end prospects heading into the draft weekend. His combination of size, speed, and route-running ability generated significant buzz among NFL evaluators throughout the pre-draft process, and Philadelphia ultimately decided he was the ideal player to invest a premium pick in on Day 2.

Who Is Eli Stowers?

Eli Stowers is a 6-foot-5, 248-pound tight end who spent his collegiate career competing in the Southeastern Conference, widely regarded as the most physically demanding conference in college football. Playing at Vanderbilt, Stowers consistently proved himself against elite competition, which only elevated his stock entering the draft cycle.

During his time with the Commodores, Stowers developed into one of the most reliable receiving options in the SEC. He demonstrated an exceptional ability to win against linebackers in the intermediate areas of the field, displayed strong hands in traffic, and showed the athleticism necessary to threaten defenses vertically down the seam. His blocking continued to improve each season, addressing what scouts considered an early weakness in his game.

Pre-Draft Evaluations and Combine Performance

Stowers generated significant attention during the pre-draft process. At the NFL Scouting Combine, he posted impressive athletic testing numbers that confirmed what scouts had observed on film throughout his college career. His speed and change of direction numbers were considered elite for the tight end position, raising his draft projection considerably in the weeks before the draft.

  • Elite receiving ability: Stowers showed consistent hands in traffic and the ability to create separation against linebackers and safeties.
  • Seam threat: His straight-line speed allows him to stress defenses vertically, a trait increasingly valued at the position in modern offenses.
  • Route running: Coaches and scouts praised his ability to run a complete route tree, uncommon for players his size.
  • Blocking development: While not a dominant run blocker, he showed significant growth as a functional in-line blocker throughout his college career.
  • Football IQ: Multiple NFL coaches who met with Stowers during pre-draft visits commented on his preparation and understanding of offensive schemes.

What the Eagles Get With This Pick

For the Eagles, this selection addresses a positional need the organization has been working to solve. Philadelphia's offense has long relied on versatile weapons to keep opposing defenses off balance, and a tight end of Stowers' caliber provides the kind of mismatch creator that can elevate an entire offensive system.

In today's NFL, the tight end position has become one of the most valuable on the field. Elite tight ends who can both receive and block give offensive coordinators significant flexibility in game planning, allowing them to create favorable matchups across multiple personnel groupings. Stowers fits that mold precisely.

The Eagles have been searching for a tight end who can be a genuine weapon in the passing game while also holding up physically at the point of attack. Eli Stowers represents exactly the kind of dual-threat option that changes how a defense has to prepare for you each week.

Fitting Into the Eagles' Offensive System

Philadelphia's offensive system has historically made strong use of the tight end position. Stowers will have the opportunity to learn from experienced veterans in the locker room and develop within a system that will ask him to contribute in multiple ways from the moment he arrives at training camp.

The Eagles' coaching staff is expected to ease Stowers into the offense while giving him ample opportunity to showcase his receiving ability early in his rookie season. Given his athleticism and polish as a route runner, there is a genuine expectation within the organization that he could make an immediate impact rather than requiring a lengthy development period.

His versatility is particularly appealing. Stowers can line up in-line, in the slot, or as a detached receiver, giving the offense the ability to use him in a wide variety of formations and situations. That positional flexibility is something offensive coordinators covet in modern NFL tight ends, and it is a primary reason the Eagles were willing to commit a second-round pick to the position.

The Vanderbilt Connection

Stowers joins a growing list of SEC tight ends who have made successful transitions to the professional level. Playing in the SEC prepared him for the physical demands of professional football in a way that few other college conferences can match. Week in and week out, Stowers faced some of the best defensive talent in college football, and he consistently performed at a high level.

Vanderbilt's program may not carry the same national prestige as some of the conference's powerhouse programs, but it has proven capable of producing NFL-caliber talent. Stowers embraced the challenge of competing in a difficult environment and used it to accelerate his development. Eagles scouts who followed him throughout the season noted that his production remained consistent even in the most challenging matchups he faced.

Long-Term Outlook for Stowers in Philadelphia

The Eagles are investing in Stowers not just for the immediate future but with the belief that he can grow into one of the better tight ends in the NFC. Second-round picks at the tight end position historically take one to two seasons to fully establish themselves at the NFL level, but the expectation in Philadelphia is that Stowers' advanced college experience will accelerate that timeline.

His contract will keep him in Philadelphia on a cost-controlled deal for the foreseeable future, allowing the Eagles to continue building around him as he develops. If Stowers reaches the ceiling scouts believe is possible, the Eagles will have found exceptional value in the second round of the 2026 draft.

Eagles Continue Active Draft Weekend

The Stowers selection was part of what has been a productive draft weekend for the Eagles organization. Philadelphia's front office came into the 2026 draft with clear needs to address and has been methodical in targeting players who fit both the team's schematic requirements and its broader organizational culture.

Adding a prospect with Stowers' upside in the second round represents exactly the kind of value-driven approach the Eagles have embraced in recent draft cycles. Rather than reaching for a player at a premium position in the first round, Philadelphia was patient and rewarded for it by landing one of the draft's most intriguing tight end prospects with a pick that carries significantly less financial risk.

As Stowers prepares to make the transition to the professional level, all indications suggest he is ready for the challenge. His work ethic, his intelligence, and his physical tools have drawn consistent praise from everyone who has evaluated him throughout the pre-draft process. For Eagles fans looking for a reason to be excited about the future of the offense, Eli Stowers may be exactly what they have been waiting for at the tight end position.
