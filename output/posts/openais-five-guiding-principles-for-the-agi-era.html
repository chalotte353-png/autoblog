OpenAI's Five Guiding Principles for the AGI Era

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman has outlined five foundational principles that govern the company's pursuit of artificial general intelligence. These guiding values reflect the organization's commitment to ensuring that transformative AI technology serves the interests of all people on Earth. The principles come at a pivotal moment as AGI development accelerates across the global technology landscape.
Alex Rivera
Tech Editor
· April 28, 2026 · 5 min read

OpenAI Sets the Stage With a Clear Philosophical Framework

On April 28, 2026, OpenAI published a landmark document titled "Our Principles," authored by CEO Sam Altman, outlining the five core values that guide every major decision the company makes. As artificial general intelligence transitions from theoretical concept to operational reality, OpenAI is making a deliberate effort to communicate to the public, policymakers, and the broader technology community exactly what the organization stands for and how it intends to navigate one of the most consequential technological developments in human history.

The publication arrives at a moment of heightened global attention on AI safety, governance, and accountability. Governments around the world are drafting or refining legislation to regulate advanced AI systems, and the stakes have never been higher. In this context, Altman's five principles serve not only as an internal compass but as a public declaration of intent.

The Mission Behind the Principles

OpenAI's foundational mission remains unchanged: to ensure that artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity. Every principle Altman outlines flows directly from this mission statement. What distinguishes this latest articulation is its specificity and its candid acknowledgment that building AGI is no longer a distant aspiration but an active, ongoing project with real-world implications unfolding in real time.

Altman has been vocal in recent months about the responsibility that comes with being at the frontier of AGI development. In the published document, he writes with measured urgency, recognizing that the decisions OpenAI makes today will shape the trajectory of this technology for decades to come. The five principles are designed to ensure that commercial pressures, competitive dynamics, and the sheer speed of progress do not compromise the organization's core commitments.

Breaking Down the Five Principles

1. Broadly Beneficial Outcomes for All of Humanity

The first and most foundational principle asserts that OpenAI's work must produce outcomes that are broadly beneficial, not just for a select group of people, nations, or corporate interests. Altman emphasizes that "all of humanity" is not rhetorical flourish but a genuine operational constraint. This means the company must continually ask whether its products and research serve the many or the few, and course-correct whenever the answer begins to skew toward the latter.

This principle also implies a global perspective. OpenAI has increasingly engaged with international partners, academic institutions, and civil society organizations to ensure that its vision of beneficial AGI is not inadvertently shaped by the cultural or economic assumptions of any single geography.

2. Long-Term Safety as a Non-Negotiable Priority

Safety is the second principle, and Altman frames it not as a constraint on progress but as a prerequisite for it. OpenAI believes that an AGI system that is powerful but unsafe is worse than no AGI at all. The company has invested heavily in alignment research, interpretability tools, and red-teaming protocols to identify failure modes before they become catastrophic.

"We believe that the risks of AGI that are catastrophic or irreversible must be avoided, even if it means slowing down our own development timeline."

This commitment to long-term safety distinguishes OpenAI from some of its competitors, who have been criticized for prioritizing capability benchmarks over robust safety evaluations. Altman's document makes clear that no capability milestone justifies bypassing the safety review process.

3. Technical Leadership at the Frontier

The third principle may surprise those who view OpenAI primarily through a safety lens. Altman argues that in order to shape how AGI is developed globally, OpenAI must remain at the frontier of the technology. A company that falls behind technically loses its ability to influence norms, standards, and practices across the industry.

This principle reflects a strategic reality: if OpenAI stepped back from frontier research in the name of caution, other actors less committed to safety and broad benefit would simply fill the void. Leadership at the frontier is therefore framed as a responsibility, not a competitive obsession.

4. Collaborative Engagement With the World

The fourth principle centers on collaboration. OpenAI explicitly rejects the notion that AGI development should happen behind closed doors, insulated from public scrutiny or democratic input. Altman outlines a commitment to engaging with governments, researchers, ethicists, civil society groups, and everyday users to ensure that the development of AGI reflects a wide range of perspectives and values.

This principle is operationalized through initiatives such as OpenAI's policy engagement programs, its academic research partnerships, and its ongoing efforts to make certain tools and findings publicly available. The company acknowledges that it does not have all the answers and that meaningful collaboration is essential to getting this right.

  • Policy engagement: Working directly with legislators and regulators to inform sensible AI governance frameworks.
  • Academic partnerships: Collaborating with universities and independent research institutions on alignment and interpretability research.
  • Public transparency: Publishing safety evaluations, model cards, and usage policies to keep the broader community informed.

5. Responsible Deployment and Ongoing Accountability

The fifth and final principle addresses how OpenAI deploys its systems into the world. Altman makes clear that the act of releasing a powerful AI model carries ongoing obligations. OpenAI commits to monitoring how its systems are used, responding to misuse, iterating on safety measures post-deployment, and being transparent when things go wrong.

Accountability is built into this principle in a meaningful way. Rather than treating deployment as the end of the responsibility chain, OpenAI views it as the beginning of a new phase of stewardship. This includes maintaining robust content policies, investing in trust and safety infrastructure, and being willing to restrict or modify access to systems when evidence of harm emerges.

Why These Principles Matter Now

The publication of these principles is timely for several reasons. The pace of AI capability advancement has outstripped many early predictions, and the gap between what AI systems can do and what society is prepared to handle is widening. Regulatory frameworks are still catching up, and public understanding of what AGI actually means remains limited and often distorted by science fiction tropes.

By articulating a clear set of principles, OpenAI is attempting to model the kind of responsible AI leadership it believes the entire industry needs to adopt. Whether competitors follow suit remains to be seen, but the document represents a meaningful contribution to the ongoing global conversation about how humanity should govern its most powerful technologies.

For those interested in reading the full document, OpenAI has made it available on their official website, where Altman elaborates on each principle in his own words.

Looking Ahead

Altman concludes the document with a note of cautious optimism. He acknowledges that the path to beneficial AGI is uncertain and that OpenAI will inevitably face difficult tradeoffs. But he expresses confidence that by holding firmly to these five principles, the organization can navigate those challenges without losing sight of what it is ultimately trying to achieve.

The world will be watching closely. As AGI development continues to accelerate, the principles that guide the most influential organizations in this space will determine not just the fate of individual companies, but the shape of the future itself. OpenAI's declaration is a significant step toward the kind of principled, transparent, and accountable AI development that the moment demands.
