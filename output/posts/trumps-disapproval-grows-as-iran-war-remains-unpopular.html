Trump's Disapproval Grows as Iran War Remains Unpopular

NBC News poll shows Trump's approval rating drops to 37% as Americans grow weary of Iran conflict amid ongoing peace negotiations.

Trump's Approval Rating Plummets to 37% as Iran Conflict Drags On

A new NBC News poll released this week reveals deepening public disapproval of President Donald Trump's handling of the Iran crisis, with his job approval rating standing at just 37% among American adults. The survey, conducted over a five-day period, underscores growing voter fatigue with the prolonged military engagement and ongoing diplomatic negotiations with Tehran.

Public Sentiment Shifts Against Extended Conflict

The declining approval numbers reflect a significant shift in American public opinion regarding U.S. military involvement in the Middle East. According to the NBC News findings, 63% of respondents now disapprove of Trump's job performance, marking one of the lowest approval ratings of his second term. The poll surveyed approximately 1,000 registered voters across all demographic categories and political affiliations.

The sustained military presence in the region, coupled with the absence of a decisive resolution, has worn heavily on public sentiment. Analysts point to several factors contributing to the erosion of support:

  • Economic concerns related to military spending and potential oil market disruptions
  • Humanitarian concerns about civilian casualties and regional instability
  • Fatigue from protracted military engagements without clear strategic objectives
  • Partisan divisions over foreign policy approach and negotiation tactics

Peace Negotiations Stall as Tensions Persist

The Trump administration has been attempting to broker a comprehensive peace agreement with Iran through intensive diplomatic channels. However, negotiations have proceeded slowly, with both sides maintaining hardline positions on key issues including nuclear capabilities, regional proxy activities, and economic sanctions relief.

Sources close to the negotiations indicate that progress has stalled on several critical fronts, particularly regarding verification mechanisms and timeline for sanctions reduction. The lack of tangible progress in recent weeks has further diminished public confidence in the administration's foreign policy strategy.

Demographic Breakdown Reveals Deep Divides

The poll results show stark differences in approval ratings across demographic and political lines. Independent voters express the highest disapproval of the Iran strategy, with 71% disapproving of Trump's handling of the situation. Democratic voters show even higher disapproval rates at 88%, while Republican support remains relatively stable at 68% approval, though this represents a decline from earlier in the year.

Age also plays a significant role in shaping opinion. Younger voters aged 18-34 demonstrate the strongest opposition to continued military engagement, with 76% disapproving of current policy. Older voters aged 65 and above show more divided sentiment, with 52% disapproving—still a significant plurality against the administration's approach.

Economic Impact Weighs on Public Perception

Economists warn that the ongoing military presence has begun to impact domestic economic indicators. Oil prices have experienced volatility, and military expenditures have diverted resources from domestic priorities. When poll respondents were asked about their primary concerns, the economy ranked highest at 34%, with foreign policy and military engagement closely following at 28%.

The combination of economic uncertainty and military spending has created a precarious political environment for the administration. Independent analysts suggest that approval ratings could decline further if economic indicators deteriorate or if the conflict intensifies without diplomatic resolution.

Congressional Pressure Mounting

Members of Congress from both parties have begun openly questioning the administration's Iran strategy. Several Republican senators have called for a clear exit timeline and defined diplomatic objectives, while Democratic lawmakers have demanded immediate cessation of hostilities and return to multilateral negotiations.

House committees have scheduled hearings to examine the administration's military and diplomatic strategy, with bipartisan support for increased congressional oversight of Iran-related decisions. This legislative pressure reflects broader public discontent measured in the NBC News survey.

Administration's Response and Path Forward

White House officials have defended the current approach, arguing that strong military posture is necessary to achieve favorable negotiation outcomes. Press Secretary spokespersons have emphasized ongoing diplomatic efforts and suggested that public opinion may shift once a peace agreement is finalized.

However, administration insiders acknowledge the political challenge posed by declining approval ratings. Political strategists within the White House are reportedly exploring options to accelerate negotiations or demonstrate measurable progress before midterm campaign season fully begins.

Looking Ahead

The NBC News poll results suggest that the Iran conflict will remain a central political issue through the 2026 midterm elections. Voter disapproval of Trump's handling of the situation could impact Republican candidates in competitive districts and states, particularly in areas with significant military populations or economic dependence on avoiding Middle East conflicts.

As negotiations continue behind closed doors, both American policymakers and the Iranian government face pressure to reach a settlement. For President Trump, the political calculus is clear: sustained public disapproval could embolden political opponents and complicate the administration's legislative agenda on domestic priorities.

The path forward remains uncertain, but one conclusion appears inescapable—American voters are increasingly weary of prolonged military engagement and eager for definitive diplomatic resolution. Whether the Trump administration can deliver such resolution before further erosion of public support remains one of the defining challenges of 2026.
