WWE WrestleMania 42 Night 2: Full Results, Winners And Match Grades

The curtain has fallen on what many are already calling one of the most memorable WrestleManias in recent history. WWE WrestleMania 42 Night 2 — branded as WrestleMania Sunday — unfolded on April 19, 2026, before a sold-out crowd of over 72,000 fans at Allegiant Stadium in Las Vegas, Nevada, delivering a night of high-stakes championship matches, jaw-dropping surprises, and emotional storyline payoffs that lived up to the premium price of admission.

Following a strong Night 1 on Saturday, April 18, the pressure was squarely on WWE's creative and in-ring talent to close out the weekend on the highest possible note. By nearly every measure, they succeeded. Here is a complete breakdown of every match, result, and grade from WrestleMania Sunday.

WrestleMania 42 Night 2 Match Results And Grades

Opening Match: Women's United States Championship — Jade Cargill (c) vs. Bayley

The show opened with an explosive encounter between Jade Cargill and a returning Bayley, who had positioned herself as the sentimental favorite heading into Vegas. Cargill, the dominant and physically imposing champion, used her power advantage early, but Bayley countered with ring intelligence built over a decade at the top of WWE's women's division.

After a sequence of near-falls that had the crowd on its feet, Jade Cargill retained the Women's United States Championship following a staggering Jaded finishing maneuver. The match ran approximately 14 minutes and served as a legitimate statement opener.

Grade: B+ — A focused, well-paced opener that established the energy of the evening. Cargill continues to evolve as a believable top-tier champion, while Bayley's performance reminded the audience why she remains one of wrestling's most reliable performers.

Tag Team Championship Ladder Match — The New Day vs. The Bloodline vs. Motor City Machine Guns (c) vs. DIY

Few match types are more reliably spectacular at WrestleMania than the ladder match, and this four-way contest for the WWE Tag Team Championships was no exception. All eight competitors — spanning multiple generations of tag team excellence — brought an urgency and physicality that elevated the match from spectacle to genuine art form.

The Bloodline, represented by Jacob Fatu and Tama Tonga, dominated the middle portion of the match, while DIY's Tommaso Ciampa and Johnny Gargano provided the emotional throughline, teasing a reunion triumph that had the crowd emotionally invested. Ultimately, however, it was The New Day — Kofi Kingston and Xavier Woods — who retrieved the titles, delivering a storybook moment as Kingston's breathtaking mid-air ladder catch became an instant WrestleMania highlight.

Grade: A- — Spectacular. The ladder match formula rarely disappoints, and this iteration featured several genuinely original sequences. New Day's title win felt earned and emotionally resonant.

Singles Match: Kevin Owens vs. Sami Zayn

No rivalry in WWE carries more layered history than Owens versus Zayn, and their WrestleMania 42 chapter proved worthy of that legacy. Built on months of betrayal, friendship rekindled and fractured again, the match was less a wrestling contest and more a theatrical masterclass.

Kevin Owens defeated Sami Zayn via pinfall following a Pop-Up Powerbomb onto the ring steps, an ending that drew legitimate heat from a crowd that had clearly been rooting for Zayn throughout. Post-match, Owens offered a handshake in a moment of ambiguous moral complexity that WWE expertly left unresolved.

Grade: A — The best storytelling match of the night. Owens and Zayn are incapable of having a bad match when the stakes are this high. A future classic in their long, storied series.

Women's World Championship — Rhea Ripley (c) vs. Liv Morgan vs. Becky Lynch

The marquee women's match of Night 2 delivered exactly the fireworks that had been promised. A triple threat involving three of WWE's most compelling characters, the match benefited from near-perfect pacing and a crowd that was loudly invested in all three outcomes simultaneously.

Liv Morgan brought chaos and unpredictability. Becky Lynch brought technique and crowd adoration. Rhea Ripley brought power and the quiet confidence of a dominant champion. After a breathtaking final sequence in which all three traded finishing attempts within 90 seconds, Rhea Ripley retained the Women's World Championship, pinning Liv Morgan following a Riptide to close out a genuinely excellent match.

Grade: A- — WWE's women's division continues to be a legitimate driver of the product's premium feel. All three performers were outstanding, and Ripley's retention felt like the correct call at this stage of her championship reign.

Undisputed WWE Championship — Cody Rhodes (c) vs. Roman Reigns

The main event of WrestleMania 42 was, in every conceivable way, the culmination of years of narrative investment. Cody Rhodes, the reigning Undisputed WWE Champion, faced Roman Reigns in a rematch steeped in history, personal animosity, and the weight of legacy on both sides.

Reigns, returning to WrestleMania with renewed focus and a retooled character stripped of The Bloodline's overbearing interference, was arguably the best version of himself presented to a WrestleMania audience in years. Rhodes, meanwhile, carried himself with the quiet dignity of a champion who understands the magnitude of his position.

The 38-minute contest was the longest match of the weekend and the most complete. It featured a mid-match referee bump, a shocking near-fall off a spear through the announce table, and a closing stretch that produced three consecutive kick-outs from Cody Rhodes that rendered the audience nearly speechless. In the end, Cody Rhodes retained the Undisputed WWE Championship following a Cross Rhodes delivered atop the steel steps.

Post-match, the two men shared a moment of mutual respect that felt genuinely earned — a clean, definitive ending to one of professional wrestling's most compelling multi-year arcs.

Grade: A+ — An instant WrestleMania classic. Both men delivered career-defining performances. The match stands alongside the best main events in WrestleMania history and cements Cody Rhodes' place as one of the most important professional wrestlers of his generation.

Night 2 Overall Assessment

WrestleMania 42 Night 2 was a masterclass in sports entertainment execution. WWE's ability to balance spectacle, athleticism, and genuine emotional storytelling has rarely been on clearer display than it was on Sunday night in Las Vegas.

  • Match of the Night: Cody Rhodes vs. Roman Reigns
  • Performance of the Night: Kevin Owens
  • Surprise of the Night: New Day's ladder match title win
  • Overall Night 2 Grade: A

Business Context: WrestleMania's Growing Commercial Footprint

Beyond the in-ring product, WrestleMania 42 represented a significant commercial milestone for WWE and parent company TKO Group Holdings. According to preliminary figures shared by WWE's communications team, the Las Vegas weekend generated an estimated $200 million in economic impact for the region — a record for the event's Nevada debut.

Netflix, which carries WWE's flagship programming under a landmark rights deal, reported record concurrent viewers for both nights of WrestleMania 42, further validating the strategic decision to bring WWE's premium live events to the streaming platform. The Cody Rhodes vs. Roman Reigns main event is expected to rank among Netflix's most-watched sports entertainment broadcasts since the partnership began.

TKO Group Holdings, led by Executive Chairman Vince McMahon's successors and President Nick Khan, has positioned WrestleMania as a global entertainment property that transcends traditional wrestling demographics. The presence of multiple A-list celebrity appearances throughout the weekend — including a performance by a major musical act during the WrestleMania Sunday pre-show — underscored WWE's ambitions to compete for mainstream cultural attention on the level of the Super Bowl or the Oscars.

Looking Ahead: What WrestleMania 42 Means For WWE's Immediate Future

With WrestleMania in the rearview mirror, WWE's attention shifts immediately to the post-Mania season, historically one of the most creatively unpredictable stretches on the calendar. Cody Rhodes' championship retention sets up a summer filled with potential challengers, while the New Day's tag title victory opens new chapter opportunities for the division's broader landscape.

Roman Reigns' clean WrestleMania loss, while perhaps surprising to some, positions the former Head of the Table for a fascinating character evolution heading into the summer. Whether WWE pivots him toward a babyface trajectory or doubles down on a darkened, more personal version of his established character remains one of the most intriguing questions in sports entertainment heading into the back half of 2026.

What is not in question is the quality of what was delivered over two nights in Las Vegas. WrestleMania 42 was not simply a great wrestling show — it was a great show, full stop. In a media landscape fragmented across streaming services, social platforms, and competing entertainment options, WWE's ability to make the world stop and watch remains its most valuable and irreplaceable asset.

WrestleMania 42 Night 2 is available to stream in full on Netflix. WWE's next premium live event, Backlash 2026, is scheduled for May 2026.
