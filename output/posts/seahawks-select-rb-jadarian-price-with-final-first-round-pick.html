Seahawks Select RB Jadarian Price With Final First-Round Pick

Seahawks Close First Round With Jadarian Price Selection

The Seattle Seahawks made one of the more anticipated selections of the 2026 NFL Draft on Thursday night, using the final pick of the first round to select running back Jadarian Price. The pick came after a night of reported trade discussions that ultimately went nowhere, leaving General Manager John Schneider to stay put and add what many evaluators consider one of the most dynamic backfield talents in this draft class.

The selection generated immediate buzz among fans and analysts alike, as Price brings an explosive combination of speed, power, and receiving ability that fits precisely what the Seahawks have long sought out of their backfield. Seattle now enters the offseason with a significant new weapon in their offensive arsenal heading into what promises to be a critical season for the franchise.

Schneider Sought a Trade but Couldn't Find a Partner

In the days leading up to the draft, John Schneider was candid about his intentions. The longtime Seattle general manager made clear that moving down the board was very much on his radar, a strategy the organization has employed with great success in past drafts to accumulate additional selections and roster depth.

Schneider acknowledged this week that it was no secret he wanted to trade down on Thursday night, but emphasized that it simply takes two willing parties to make a deal happen, and he was unable to find that dance partner before time expired.

Despite multiple reported conversations with teams looking to move up, no agreement could be reached that satisfied Seattle's asking price. With the clock winding down and no deal imminent, Schneider and his front office staff turned in the card for Price, and the running back's name was called to close out one of the most watched nights in professional football's annual calendar.

It is worth noting that Schneider's trade-down philosophy has been a defining characteristic of his tenure in Seattle. He has historically been one of the most active general managers when it comes to moving around the board, and the inability to execute that strategy Thursday night marked a notable deviation from the team's standard operating procedure.

Who Is Jadarian Price?

For fans unfamiliar with Price heading into draft night, the selection quickly sent scouts and analysts scrambling to pull up their notes on the highly regarded prospect. Price arrives in Seattle with a reputation as a versatile, three-down back who has the potential to step in and make an immediate impact at the NFL level.

Athletic Profile and Playing Style

Price is widely described as a physically impressive athlete who combines elite straight-line speed with an ability to make defenders miss in tight spaces. His burst through the line of scrimmage drew comparisons to some of the more productive young backs to enter the league in recent seasons, and his hands out of the backfield give offensive coordinators a reliable check-down option in the passing game.

  • Speed and Explosiveness: Price consistently tested among the top performers at his position in pre-draft workouts, displaying elite acceleration that translates to big plays in the open field.
  • Receiving Ability: His capability as a pass catcher separates him from pure between-the-tackles runners, making him a matchup problem for linebackers in coverage.
  • Vision and Patience: Scouts praised his ability to read blocking schemes and allow lanes to develop before attacking with full speed, a quality that tends to translate well to the professional game.
  • Pass Protection: One area that drew some concern among evaluators was his consistency in pass protection, though most agreed the technical aspects of that skill set can be refined with proper coaching at the next level.

Fit Within Seattle's Offensive System

The Seahawks have emphasized a physical, run-first identity in recent seasons, and adding a talent like Price only deepens that commitment. Head coach and offensive staff will now have the opportunity to build a backfield attack around Price's unique skill set, incorporating his receiving prowess into the scheme to keep defenses off balance.

Seattle's offensive line has been a point of investment in recent years, and the hope is that improved blocking up front will allow Price to hit the second level with regularity, where his speed becomes particularly difficult for opposing defenses to handle. The combination of a rebuilt offensive line and a first-round running back talent represents a significant statement of intent from the organization.

Reaction From Around the League

The reaction from analysts and former players was largely positive following Seattle's selection. Many praised the value of taking a running back of Price's caliber at that spot in the draft, noting that the position has seen a resurgence in recent years as teams recognize the importance of a ground game in controlling the tempo of games.

Some observers pointed out that while running back has been a devalued position in recent drafts, the tide has begun to shift as offenses that lean on the run have found renewed success in the modern NFL. Taking Price in the first round signals that Seattle believes a dominant backfield presence can be a foundational element of a championship-caliber offense.

The selection was viewed by many talent evaluators as a strong value play, with some noting that Price's combined rushing and receiving upside made him worthy of consideration well before the final pick of round one.

What This Means for the Seahawks Going Forward

With Price now in the fold, Seattle enters day two and day three of the draft with clarity about where their first major addition of this class will contribute. The front office can now turn its attention to addressing other areas of need, including depth along the defensive line, secondary reinforcements, and continued investment in the offensive line.

Schneider's failure to trade down will inevitably be discussed in the days ahead, but the organization appears confident that the player they selected justifies staying at that position in the draft. Whether Price lives up to the investment will only be determined once the pads go on and the regular season arrives, but on draft night, the mood in Seattle was one of excitement and optimism.

The 2026 NFL Draft continues through the weekend, and the Seahawks still hold several selections in the later rounds. Schneider and his staff will look to add complementary pieces around Price and the rest of their current roster, continuing to build toward what they hope will be a return to sustained competitiveness in the NFC West.

For now, Jadarian Price is a Seattle Seahawk, the final name called on the first night of the draft and the newest face of an offense looking to announce itself as one of the more formidable units in the conference. The journey from draft pick to proven professional starter is long and filled with challenges, but the organization has made clear with this selection that they believe Price is ready to take that first step.
