Way-Too-Early 2027 NFL Mock Draft: Three QBs in Top Five

The 2027 NFL Draft is over a year away, but the quarterback carousel is already spinning as the Cardinals, Jets, and Browns continue their searches for franchise signal-callers. Ohio State wide receiver Jeremiah Smith is emerging as a potential generational talent who could challenge the traditional quarterback-first draft logic at the very top of the board.

Football Correspondent
· April 30, 2026 · 6 min read

The 2027 NFL Draft Picture Is Already Coming Into Focus

It may only be April 2026, but the NFL landscape moves fast, and teams already looking ahead to next year's draft class are finding plenty to be excited about. The 2027 NFL Draft class is shaping up to be one of the more quarterback-rich groups in recent memory, with three signal-callers projected to land inside the top five picks. But the most fascinating storyline may not involve a quarterback at all.

Ohio State wide receiver Jeremiah Smith has quietly positioned himself as arguably the best overall prospect in the 2027 class, a rare occurrence in an era when quarterbacks almost always dominate the top of the board. His combination of size, speed, route-running precision, and contested-catch ability has scouts drawing comparisons to some of the greatest wideouts to ever play the game.

Meanwhile, three franchises that have struggled for years to find their quarterback of the future, the Arizona Cardinals, New York Jets, and Cleveland Browns, are all expected to be in prime draft position to finally address that need in a meaningful way. Here is how the way-too-early 2027 NFL Mock Draft is shaping up.

The Top Five Picks: A Quarterback Frenzy With One Major Wildcard

Pick No. 1: Arizona Cardinals

The Arizona Cardinals have cycled through quarterback options for years, and while flashes of promise have appeared at various moments, the franchise has yet to lock in a true cornerstone at the position. Heading into the 2027 draft, the Cardinals are projected to hold the first overall pick and face a genuinely difficult decision.

The top quarterback in this class is expected to be a pocket-passing prospect with elite arm talent and the football IQ to operate a complex pro-style offense from day one. If that player is available, Arizona is unlikely to pass on him. However, the presence of Jeremiah Smith as a potentially transformational wide receiver adds an unexpected layer of complexity to the selection process.

When a wide receiver genuinely challenges a quarterback for the top spot on draft boards, it speaks to just how rare that kind of talent is. Jeremiah Smith is that kind of player.

For a team like the Cardinals that has also needed playmakers at the skill positions, the temptation to take Smith and address quarterback through free agency or a later round could be real. Most projections still lean toward Arizona taking the best available quarterback, but this is a debate that will intensify throughout the pre-draft process.

Pick No. 2: New York Jets

The New York Jets have one of the most tortured quarterback histories in professional football. After years of misfires and false starts, the franchise is once again expected to be shopping for a franchise quarterback in the 2027 draft. With a likely top-two pick, the Jets are projected to land the second quarterback off the board.

The second-best signal-caller in this class is described by evaluators as a dual-threat weapon with the arm strength to make every throw and the mobility to extend plays when the pocket collapses. His floor is considered high, and his ceiling is considered elite, making him a safe pick for a franchise desperate to get the position right.

For Jets fans who have waited years for stability at quarterback, the 2027 draft could finally represent the light at the end of a very long tunnel.

Pick No. 3: Cleveland Browns

The Cleveland Browns remain one of the most quarterback-starved franchises in the NFL. Despite investing significant resources and draft capital in the position over recent years, the Browns have yet to find their long-term answer. The 2027 draft offers another opportunity.

The third quarterback projected in the top five is considered a more developmental prospect, someone who may not be ready to start immediately but whose physical tools and upside are simply too tantalizing to pass on at that spot in the draft. Cleveland, with its history of patience-testing quarterback decisions, may finally get it right by trusting the process with a high-ceiling prospect.

Pick No. 4: Tennessee Titans

The Tennessee Titans are projected to select Jeremiah Smith with the fourth overall pick, a move that would give them one of the most electrifying offensive weapons in the entire NFL. Smith's ability to win at every level of the field, whether running short slants, intermediate crossers, or deep go routes, makes him a matchup nightmare that no defensive coordinator has a clean answer for.

Pick No. 5: New England Patriots

The New England Patriots round out the top five, likely targeting either a top offensive lineman or a defensive cornerstone depending on how the board falls. With the quarterback class depth addressed in the top four, New England could pivot to strengthening the roster around whatever plans they have at the quarterback position heading into 2027.

Jeremiah Smith: The Player Redefining Draft Conversations

It is worth spending additional time on Jeremiah Smith because his emergence as a true top-five candidate at a non-quarterback position is genuinely rare in the modern NFL draft era. The Ohio State wide receiver has spent the past two seasons systematically dismantling Big Ten secondaries with a level of ease that has scouts reaching for historical comparisons.

  • Size and athleticism: Smith possesses a rare combination of height, wingspan, and explosive speed that allows him to win in multiple ways against any coverage.
  • Route running: His ability to create separation through precise footwork and deceptive release moves is considered advanced even by professional standards.
  • Contested catches: Smith excels in jump ball situations, using his body control and hand strength to win fifty-fifty opportunities at an elite rate.
  • Football IQ: Coaches and scouts consistently praise his ability to diagnose coverage pre-snap and adjust his routes accordingly.
  • Production: His statistical output at Ohio State has been consistently dominant against top competition, reinforcing the idea that his skills translate at the highest level.

The last time a wide receiver was seriously discussed as a potential first overall pick, it generated enormous debate across the football world. Smith is reigniting that conversation, and the teams picking in the top five will have to genuinely wrestle with whether his value exceeds even a top quarterback prospect.

What Teams Need to Watch Before the 2027 Draft

While this mock draft is built on current projections and player development trajectories, a great deal will change between now and the spring of 2027. The 2026 NFL season will play a massive role in determining draft order, and breakout performances or unexpected injuries could completely reshape the conversation.

Several developmental quarterbacks currently on NFL rosters could also emerge during the 2026 season, potentially reducing the urgency for teams like the Cardinals, Jets, and Browns to prioritize the position in the draft. Free agency ahead of the 2027 draft could also move established veterans to quarterback-needy teams, creating further ripple effects.

Additionally, the college football landscape will continue to evolve. Prospects who are currently early contributors at major programs could explode into first-round conversations during the 2026 season, potentially reshuffling the top of the board entirely by the time draft analysts submit their final big boards.

Early Projections Beyond the Top Five

Outside of the top five, the 2027 draft class appears to have significant depth at offensive line and edge rusher, two positions that always carry premium value in the NFL. Early projections suggest multiple interior offensive linemen with starting potential could be available in the late first round and early second round, offering excellent value for teams that address other needs at the top of the draft.

At the defensive end position, several college players are generating buzz as potential top-fifteen selections, which would make the 2027 class strong on both sides of the ball beyond just the quarterback and skill position depth.

For fans, coaches, and general managers across the league, the 2027 NFL Draft is already becoming must-watch appointment viewing more than a year before the first pick is made. That is the mark of a truly special draft class, and the presence of Jeremiah Smith alongside three legitimate top-five quarterback prospects makes this one of the most compelling early draft conversations in recent memory.

Final Thoughts

The way-too-early 2027 NFL Mock Draft tells a story of a league still desperately searching for answers at the most important position in professional sports. Three franchises with long histories of quarterback uncertainty, Arizona, New York, and Cleveland, appear poised to finally get their answers from a deep and talented draft class.

But the real headline may belong to Jeremiah Smith, a wide receiver whose generational talent is forcing teams to ask a question they rarely have to consider: is the best player available actually not a quarterback? The answer to that question will define the 2027 NFL Draft before the first snap of the 2026 season has even been played.

Stay tuned. This is only the beginning of what promises to be a fascinating draft cycle.
