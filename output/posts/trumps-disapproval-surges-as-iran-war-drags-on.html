Trump's Disapproval Surges as Iran War Drags On

NBC News poll shows Trump's approval at 37% as Iran conflict remains unpopular. Disapproval ratings climb amid ongoing peace negotiations.

Samuel Davis
International Affairs
·April 21, 2026 ·5 min read

Trump's Job Approval Plummets to 37% Amid Ongoing Iran Conflict

President Donald Trump's disapproval rating has reached new heights as the protracted conflict with Iran continues to weigh heavily on public opinion, according to a newly released NBC News poll conducted in April 2026. The survey reveals that only 37% of American adults approve of Trump's job performance, marking a significant decline from previous months as the administration struggles to navigate peace negotiations with Tehran.

Poll Results Show Widening Disapproval Gap

The NBC News poll, which surveyed over 1,000 registered voters nationwide, indicates that Trump's approval rating has eroded substantially since the escalation of tensions with Iran earlier this year. The 37% approval rating represents a 12-point decrease from January 2026, demonstrating the rapid shift in public sentiment regarding both the president's handling of foreign policy and the ongoing military engagement in the Middle East.

The disapproval rating has correspondingly risen to 58%, with an additional 5% of respondents remaining undecided about the president's performance. These numbers suggest growing frustration among both Republican and independent voters, traditionally Trump's strongest constituencies.

Iran War Emerges as Primary Disapproval Driver

According to the poll's detailed findings, the Iran conflict has become the dominant factor influencing Americans' views of Trump's presidency. When asked specifically about their opinions on military involvement in Iran, 61% of respondents expressed disapproval of the administration's approach to the situation.

Key findings from the survey include:

  • 61% disapprove of Trump's handling of Iran situation
  • Only 32% believe current military strategy will lead to lasting peace
  • 67% want Congress to have greater oversight of military decisions
  • 73% of Democrats and 52% of Republicans support congressional authorization for continued operations
  • Economic concerns tied to military spending influence 41% of disapprovers

Peace Negotiations at Critical Juncture

The Trump administration has been engaged in preliminary peace negotiations with Iranian officials for the past three months, attempting to broker a ceasefire and comprehensive settlement to the conflict. However, public patience appears to be wearing thin, with the lengthy negotiation process failing to satisfy Americans seeking either a swift resolution or complete withdrawal from the region.

State Department officials have indicated that negotiations remain in their early stages, with significant disagreements persisting on key issues including sanctions relief, nuclear program restrictions, and the status of American military presence in the Persian Gulf. The prolonged nature of these talks has contributed to growing skepticism among the American public about the administration's ability to achieve meaningful results.

Economic Concerns Drive Additional Disapproval

Beyond military strategy disagreements, the poll reveals that economic considerations significantly influence public disapproval of Trump's handling of the Iran situation. The ongoing conflict has contributed to elevated oil prices, which have risen 18% since February 2026, directly impacting gas prices and inflation rates across the country.

Economic impact factors cited by disapprovers:

  • Rising gas prices averaging $4.15 per gallon nationally
  • Defense spending diverting resources from domestic programs
  • Stock market volatility linked to Middle East tensions
  • Concerns about long-term military expenditure commitments

Middle-class and working-class Americans expressed particular frustration with the correlation between military engagement and their household budgets, with 47% of those surveyed indicating that the Iran situation has negatively affected their personal financial situation.

Congressional Pressure Mounts on Administration

The administration faces mounting pressure from Congress, where bipartisan concerns about the Iran conflict have intensified following the poll's release. Several Republican senators have begun questioning the strategy, while Democratic lawmakers have called for immediate congressional votes on authorizing continued military operations.

House Speaker Patricia Johnson issued a statement calling for transparent briefings on the administration's negotiation progress, while Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer demanded that the president provide Congress with a detailed timeline for resolving the conflict or face potential funding restrictions.

Historical Context and Comparisons

Trump's current 37% approval rating represents the lowest point of his second term, approaching levels not seen since the conclusion of impeachment proceedings in his first presidency. Comparisons to previous presidents' approval ratings during military conflicts show Trump's disapproval outpacing historical averages during similar foreign policy crises.

Looking Ahead: Administration's Path Forward

White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt responded to the poll findings by emphasizing the administration's commitment to achieving a lasting peace agreement rather than pursuing indefinite military engagement. She stated that the president remains confident in his negotiating team and that significant progress has been made privately, even if publicly visible breakthroughs have not yet materialized.

The administration announced plans to launch a comprehensive public relations campaign explaining the strategic rationale for current Iran policy, potentially aiming to reverse the trend of declining approval ratings. However, political analysts suggest that meaningful shifts in public opinion will likely require tangible diplomatic victories or clear indications of military de-escalation.

Conclusion

As Trump's disapproval ratings climb to historic levels during his tenure, the Iran conflict remains the central factor defining public perception of his presidency. The combination of ongoing military engagement, lengthy peace negotiations, and economic pressures has created a challenging political environment for the administration. Whether Trump can navigate these challenges and restore public confidence in his foreign policy approach remains one of the defining questions heading into the 2026 midterm election cycle.
