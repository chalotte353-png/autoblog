How the Rams Landed on Ty Simpson as Their QB Future

The Los Angeles Rams made one of the most talked-about picks of the 2026 NFL Draft by selecting Alabama quarterback Ty Simpson as the long-term successor to Matthew Stafford. Sean McVay and the front office conducted an exhaustive evaluation process before pulling the trigger on the Alabama signal-caller. Here is a complete breakdown of how that decision came together and what it means for the franchise going forward.
Maria Santos
Football Correspondent
· April 28, 2026 · 6 min read

The Rams Make Their Move: Selecting Ty Simpson

The Los Angeles Rams entered the 2026 NFL Draft with one of the most important organizational decisions hanging over their heads: who would eventually take the reins from the franchise cornerstone Matthew Stafford. On the night of April 24, the answer became clear when head coach Sean McVay and general manager Les Snead selected Alabama quarterback Ty Simpson, ending months of speculation and internal debate within the organization.

The pick sent immediate ripples through the league. Simpson, a 22-year-old signal-caller who spent three years developing under head coach Kalen DeBoer at Alabama, was widely viewed as a first-round talent with high-end upside. But the Rams, who have built their identity around precision and system fit under McVay, were not simply chasing raw talent. They were searching for someone capable of learning and eventually executing one of the most nuanced offensive systems in professional football.

Inside the Evaluation Process

According to multiple sources with direct knowledge of the Rams' pre-draft process, the evaluation of Ty Simpson began in earnest following the conclusion of the 2025 college football season. The Rams coaching staff attended multiple Alabama practices and film sessions, breaking down not just Simpson's physical tools but his processing speed, footwork mechanics, and ability to command a pro-style offense.

Sean McVay himself was described as particularly hands-on during the evaluation. Sources indicate the head coach flew to Tuscaloosa in January for a private workgroup session with Simpson and several Alabama offensive coaches. The visit was considered a pivotal moment in cementing the Rams' interest.

"McVay wanted to see how quickly Simpson could absorb new information and adapt. The Alabama coaching staff gave him full access, and what he saw clearly resonated," one source familiar with the meeting described.

The Rams also brought Simpson in for a formal pre-draft visit at their facility in Thousand Oaks, California. The visit lasted nearly two full days and included detailed whiteboard sessions, psychological evaluations, and extensive one-on-one conversations with key members of the offensive staff. By all accounts, Simpson performed exceptionally well and left a strong impression on everyone in the building.

What Ty Simpson Brings to the Table

Simpson arrives in Los Angeles as a polished pocket passer with notably advanced football intelligence for his age. At Alabama, he completed 67.4 percent of his passes over the course of his final two seasons, throwing for 28 touchdowns against only 7 interceptions in his senior campaign while navigating a Crimson Tide offense that leaned heavily on high-volume passing concepts.

His physical profile is also considered ideal for the professional game. Simpson stands 6 feet 3 inches and weighs 218 pounds, with arm strength that evaluators consistently graded in the upper tier of this draft class. Perhaps more importantly, he demonstrated a consistent ability to go through his progressions quickly and get the football out on time, a trait McVay prizes above almost all others in a quarterback.

Key Strengths Identified by the Rams

  • Pre-snap recognition: Simpson showed elite ability to identify defensive coverages before the snap and adjust protection calls accordingly.
  • Ball placement on intermediate routes: His accuracy between the numbers on crossing routes and slants was rated among the best in the class.
  • Composure under pressure: Despite playing in high-stakes SEC environments, Simpson rarely forced throws into tight coverage when the primary read was unavailable.
  • Leadership qualities: Teammates and coaches in Tuscaloosa consistently praised his work ethic and his ability to hold the locker room together during adversity.
  • System familiarity: Alabama's spread concepts under DeBoer share conceptual DNA with McVay's scheme, giving Simpson a meaningful head start.

The Stafford Succession Plan

Matthew Stafford, now 38 years old, remains under contract with the Rams and is still considered a capable starter. The organization has been transparent in stating that Stafford will continue in his role as the starter for the foreseeable future. The Simpson selection is not a signal that Stafford is finished but rather a proactive step to ensure the franchise does not face the same uncertainty at quarterback that plagued multiple teams heading into this offseason.

The Rams have watched other franchises scramble to address quarterback vacuums at the worst possible times, and McVay and Snead have consistently prioritized forward thinking in their roster construction. Selecting a developmental quarterback while Stafford is still performing at a high level gives Simpson the luxury of time, something that has proven critical in the development of modern quarterbacks entering complex offensive systems.

Simpson is expected to spend his rookie season as the clear backup, soaking in McVay's system while contributing in limited packages. The team is not placing any immediate pressure on the young passer to perform, which most evaluators believe is the correct approach given the complexity of what the Rams ask of their quarterbacks.

Standout Picks Across the 2026 Draft Class

Beyond the Rams' headline selection, the 2026 NFL Draft produced a number of notable picks that deserve attention as teams begin the process of integrating new talent into their rosters.

Offensive Playmakers Who Could Make Immediate Impact

Several wide receivers and tight ends selected in the first two rounds drew rave reviews from scouts for their combination of athleticism and route-running refinement. The class as a whole was considered particularly deep at the skill positions, giving multiple franchises the opportunity to significantly upgrade their passing games without spending premium picks.

At running back, the draft produced one of the deepest classes in recent memory. Teams that have historically undervalued the position found tremendous value in the middle rounds, with several backs showing the three-down versatility that modern offenses demand.

Defensive Standouts Worth Watching

The 2026 class was equally impressive on the defensive side of the ball. Edge rushers with the kind of bend and burst that translates most reliably from college to the professional level were available throughout the first two days of the draft, giving several teams the opportunity to bolster their pass rush without reaching in the first round.

  • Cornerback depth: This was considered a historically strong year for the position, with evaluators noting that the top ten corners in the class would have all been first-round picks in most prior years.
  • Interior defensive line: Teams in need of three-technique upgrades had exceptional options available on Day 2, reflecting the growth of the position in college football.
  • Safety class: Box safeties with legitimate coverage ability were unusually plentiful, addressing a need that multiple contending rosters have struggled with in recent seasons.

What Happens Next for the Rams

With the draft now complete, the Rams turn their attention to the rookie minicamp scheduled for early May, where Simpson and the rest of the team's draft class will get their first extended look at the professional game. The coaching staff is expected to implement a measured onboarding process for the young quarterback, prioritizing understanding over performance during those early reps.

The franchise is clearly building toward something. The decision to invest a meaningful pick in a developmental quarterback while still competing for championships reflects the organizational philosophy that has defined McVay's tenure in Los Angeles. The Rams have never been willing to simply accept a talent cliff; instead, they manage transitions before they become crises.

For Ty Simpson, the journey is just beginning. He arrives in a city with high expectations, under a coaching staff known for developing talent, and in a role that does not demand immediate perfection. If the evaluation process that led to his selection is any indication, the Rams believe they have found their quarterback of the future. Now the work of proving that belief correct begins in earnest.

Final Thoughts on the 2026 Draft Class

The 2026 NFL Draft will be remembered for its exceptional depth at multiple positions and for several franchise-altering decisions made across the first two rounds. The Rams' selection of Ty Simpson stands as one of the most strategically significant picks of the entire event, not because of what Simpson will do immediately, but because of what the pick represents for an organization that has never been content to simply manage the present at the expense of the future.

As training camps open later this summer and rosters begin to take shape, the true impact of this draft class will start to come into focus. For now, the narrative belongs to the players and the franchises that bet on them, and few bets this week carry more long-term significance than the one the Los Angeles Rams made on the young man from Tuscaloosa.
