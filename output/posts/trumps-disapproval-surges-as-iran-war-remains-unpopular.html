Trump's Disapproval Rating Grows As Iran War Remains Unpopular, Poll Finds

President Donald Trump's job approval rating has hit a significant low of 37%, according to a new NBC News poll released this week, as the ongoing Iran conflict continues to erode public confidence in his administration's foreign policy strategy. The survey reveals growing disapproval across demographic lines, with only marginally higher support among Republican voters, signaling a potential vulnerability heading into what political observers describe as a critical period for the administration.

The Approval Crisis Deepens

The NBC News poll, conducted among a nationally representative sample of 900 adults, found that 37% of respondents approve of Trump's job performance—a metric that has steadily declined over the past six months as military operations in Iran have intensified. The disapproval rating now stands at 56%, with 7% expressing no opinion, according to the poll's findings released Monday morning.

This marks a substantial shift from the president's standing in early 2026, when approval ratings hovered in the low-to-mid 40s. The deterioration comes as the White House attempts to navigate complex peace negotiations with Iranian officials while maintaining military readiness in the region—a delicate balancing act that appears to be failing with the general public.

Iran War: A National Divisive Issue

The unpopularity of military operations in Iran represents perhaps the most significant political liability for the Trump administration. Unlike previous foreign policy initiatives, the Iran conflict has generated unusual bipartisan skepticism, with majorities of Democrats, Independents, and a substantial portion of Republicans expressing concerns about the prolonged engagement.

According to the NBC News poll, 64% of adults say they disapprove of how Trump is handling the Iran situation. When asked whether the United States should continue military operations, only 31% responded affirmatively, while 59% said the country should pursue diplomatic solutions exclusively. These numbers suggest that public patience is wearing thin after months of escalating tensions and military deployments.

Trump's Disapproval Surges as Iran War Drags On, with economic concerns compounding the administration's challenges. The ongoing military expenditures have contributed to inflationary pressures that have rippled through American households, creating additional dissatisfaction with Trump's stewardship of the economy.

Economic Headwinds and Voter Sentiment

The convergence of foreign policy concerns and domestic economic anxiety appears to be driving the sharp decline in the president's approval numbers. Inflation, which economists had predicted would moderate by mid-2026, has remained stubbornly elevated due in part to military spending related to Iran operations. Consumer confidence indices have weakened accordingly, and approval of Trump's handling of the economy has fallen to 41%—down from 48% in January.

Financial markets have reflected this uncertainty, with some analysts attributing increased volatility to concerns about the administration's ability to manage both foreign policy and domestic economic challenges simultaneously. The combination of geopolitical risk and economic headwinds has created an unusually challenging political environment for the White House.

Demographic Breakdowns Reveal Widespread Disapproval

The NBC News poll provides detailed demographic breakdowns that illustrate the breadth of Trump's approval crisis:

  • Among men: 42% approve, 51% disapprove
  • Among women: 32% approve, 60% disapprove
  • Among college-educated voters: 28% approve, 65% disapprove
  • Among voters without college degrees: 44% approve, 49% disapprove
  • Among Republicans: 71% approve, 22% disapprove
  • Among Democrats: 5% approve, 92% disapprove
  • Among Independents: 30% approve, 62% disapprove

Perhaps most troubling for the administration, Trump's approval among women has reached historic lows, with only 32% expressing approval. This represents a 14-point decline from his approval rating among women just three months ago, suggesting that messaging on the Iran conflict and its human costs has particularly resonated with female voters.

Peace Negotiations at a Critical Juncture

The White House has indicated that peace negotiations with Iran have reached a critical juncture, with administration officials suggesting that a framework agreement could be finalized within weeks. Secretary of State Marco Rubio has been leading diplomatic efforts, but progress has been halting and publicly contentious, with both sides accusing the other of intransigence.

The administration has struggled to communicate a clear narrative about its Iran strategy to the American public. Critics argue that the president's messaging has been inconsistent—at times appearing bellicose, at other times suggesting imminent diplomatic breakthroughs—creating confusion among voters about the true state of affairs and the administration's ultimate objectives.

Political Implications for 2026 and Beyond

With congressional midterm elections scheduled for November 2026, Trump's deteriorating approval rating presents significant challenges for Republican candidates seeking to maintain control of Congress. Historical patterns suggest that presidents with approval ratings below 40% typically face substantial losses in midterm elections, and current political conditions offer no obvious path to rapid improvement in Trump's standing.

Democratic strategists are actively preparing campaigns that emphasize what they characterize as mismanagement of the Iran crisis and economic stewardship. Meanwhile, some establishment Republican figures have begun privately expressing concerns about the political sustainability of the Iran operations, particularly if casualties continue to mount or diplomatic progress stalls further.

Looking Ahead

The NBC News poll suggests that public opinion on the Iran conflict is relatively firm rather than volatile, indicating that short-term political messaging initiatives are unlikely to produce dramatic shifts in approval ratings. The administration's best path forward likely involves achieving a diplomatically credible peace agreement that allows for a draw-down of military operations—a development that could materially improve Trump's political standing.

Until such an agreement materializes, however, expect to see continued erosion of the president's approval ratings and sustained Republican concern about the political implications of ongoing military operations in Iran. The next several weeks will be decisive in determining whether the Trump administration can salvage its foreign policy standing and restore public confidence in its judgment on matters of war and peace.
