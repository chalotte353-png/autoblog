Sabres Stun Bruins 4-3 in Dramatic Game 1 Comeback

Sabres Complete Furious Comeback to Steal Game 1 from Bruins

BUFFALO, N.Y. — April 21, 2026 — In a game that had the KeyBank Center crowd oscillating between despair and disbelief, the Buffalo Sabres mounted a dramatic late-game comeback to defeat the Boston Bruins 4-3 in Game 1 of their first-round Stanley Cup Playoff series. The final buzzer set off a thunderous celebration inside an arena that had been waiting years for a postseason moment like this.

Tage Thompson, Buffalo's franchise centerpiece, and defenseman Mattias Samuelsson were the driving forces behind the rally, combining for leadership, physicality, and timely scoring that ultimately broke the Bruins' resolve. The Sabres, who trailed entering the final period, found another gear when it mattered most and delivered a statement victory to open the series.

How the Game Unfolded

Boston came out assertive and structured in the opening period, controlling puck possession and generating sustained pressure in the offensive zone. The Bruins struck first, converting on an early power play opportunity to take a 1-0 lead. Their veteran core looked calm and composed, appearing intent on dictating the pace of the game from the outset.

Buffalo answered before the first period expired, with Thompson winning a puck battle along the boards and feeding a linemate for a tap-in that tied the game heading into the first intermission. The goal was a signal of what Thompson intended to bring to this series — relentless competition and an ability to manufacture offense in tight spaces.

The second period saw Boston reassert itself. The Bruins scored twice in a span of five minutes, building a 3-1 lead that had Buffalo's bench searching for answers. Boston's defensive structure was suffocating, and their transition game was sharp, limiting the Sabres' speed through the neutral zone. Heading into the third period trailing by two goals, the mountain in front of Buffalo looked steep.

Thompson and Samuelsson Engineer the Turnaround

What followed in the third period will be remembered as one of the defining stretches of Buffalo's 2026 playoff run. Thompson, who has developed into one of the most dominant centers in the NHL, elevated his game to another level entirely.

He scored the Sabres' second goal of the night just over three minutes into the final frame, a powerful wrist shot from the top of the left circle that beat the Bruins' goaltender cleanly on the blocker side. The goal reignited the crowd and visibly energized his teammates on the bench.

Samuelsson, the towering defenseman who has become one of the most reliable two-way blueliners in the Eastern Conference, made his presence felt in multiple ways throughout the third period. He delivered several key defensive plays to keep Buffalo within a goal, but his most consequential contribution came with just under eight minutes remaining. Pinching aggressively from the point, Samuelsson collected a loose puck in the high slot and ripped a shot through traffic to tie the game 3-3. The KeyBank Center erupted.

With momentum firmly in Buffalo's favor, the Sabres continued to press. Boston attempted to regroup and reclaim the lead, but the Bruins were visibly rattled, no longer able to execute the patient, structured game plan that had worked so well through the first two periods.

The Winning Goal

The decisive moment arrived with 1:47 remaining in regulation. Following a sustained shift in the offensive zone, Buffalo created chaos in front of the Boston net. A scramble ensued after a save attempt, and a Sabres forward buried the rebound to make it 4-3. The goal was reviewed briefly but confirmed, and KeyBank Center reached a decibel level that shook the building to its foundation.

Boston pulled its goaltender immediately and pressed furiously in the final minutes, but Buffalo's defense held firm. The final buzzer sounded, and the Sabres spilled off the bench in celebration of a victory that had seemed improbable just 20 minutes earlier.

What This Means for the Series

Stealing Game 1 on home ice is significant not just for the standings, but for the psychological landscape of the series. The Sabres have demonstrated to Boston and to themselves that they can absorb adversity, sustain belief, and execute under pressure. Those are qualities that define playoff teams.

For the Bruins, the loss raises urgent questions about their ability to maintain composure and protect leads. They held a two-goal advantage entering the third period of a playoff opener and could not hold on. Adjustments will need to be made before Game 2.

Key Takeaways from Game 1

  • Tage Thompson finished with one goal and two assists, reminding the hockey world why he is considered among the elite centers in the game today.
  • Mattias Samuelsson provided the tying goal and was a steadying physical presence throughout, logging heavy minutes and winning critical puck battles.
  • Buffalo's third period was one of the most dominant final frames in recent Sabres postseason history, outscoring Boston 3-0 after the second intermission.
  • Boston's power play went one for four on the night, and their ability to capitalize with the man advantage will be a storyline to monitor going forward.
  • KeyBank Center provided an atmosphere befitting a playoff opener, with the building's energy clearly playing a role in Buffalo's resilience.

Voices from the Locker Room

Thompson was measured but confident when addressing reporters following the final buzzer. He credited his teammates for staying composed when Boston appeared to have the game well in hand, noting that the group never stopped believing a comeback was possible.

Samuelsson echoed that sentiment, pointing to the character built throughout the regular season as the foundation for moments like the one the Sabres experienced in Game 1. He acknowledged that executing under playoff pressure is a different challenge entirely, and that Buffalo passed an important early test.

Buffalo's head coach praised the team's mental fortitude and specifically highlighted the defensive structure that prevented Boston from extending the lead further during their most dominant stretches of play in the second period.

Looking Ahead to Game 2

Game 2 of the series is scheduled for Thursday evening at KeyBank Center, with Buffalo now holding home-ice advantage and a wave of momentum. The Bruins will need to respond with a far more complete 60-minute effort if they hope to level the series before it shifts to Boston.

Whether Game 1 proves to be the turning point of an extended series or simply a memorable individual chapter remains to be written. What is already certain is that the Buffalo Sabres, long absent from the playoff stage, have arrived with something to say. Tage Thompson, Mattias Samuelsson, and an entire organization that has endured years of patience made that abundantly clear on April 21, 2026.

The series is very much alive, and hockey fans everywhere are paying close attention to what promises to be one of the most compelling matchups of the first round.
