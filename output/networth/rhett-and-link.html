📖 Full Biography

Who Are Rhett and Link?

Rhett McLaughlin and Link Neal are two of the most enduring and successful content creators in the history of YouTube. Known professionally as Rhett and Link, this comedic duo has been best friends since elementary school in Buies Creek, North Carolina — a friendship that has spanned decades and ultimately transformed into one of the most recognizable brands in digital entertainment. Together, they host Good Mythical Morning, a daily YouTube talk show that has captivated tens of millions of fans around the world, affectionately known as 'Mythical Beasts.'

Early Life and Lifelong Friendship

Rhett James McLaughlin was born on October 11, 1977, and Charles Lincoln 'Link' Neal III was born on June 1, 1978. The two met in first grade at Buies Creek Elementary School in North Carolina under somewhat legendary circumstances — both were assigned detention on the same day, sparking a friendship that would last a lifetime. Growing up in a small Southern town, the pair bonded over shared humor, creativity, and a love of making people laugh.

Both went on to attend North Carolina State University, where they studied engineering — Rhett earning a degree in civil engineering and Link in industrial engineering. Despite their technical academic backgrounds, their passion for entertainment never faded, and they began creating comedy sketches and short films during their college years.

The Rise of a YouTube Empire

Rhett and Link's journey to YouTube stardom didn't happen overnight. They began posting content online in the mid-2000s, creating viral videos, local commercial parodies, and sketch comedy that slowly built a loyal audience. Their quirky local commercial parodies became a particular hit, earning them early media attention and demonstrating their unique comedic voice.

In January 2012, they launched Good Mythical Morning (GMM), a daily morning talk show format that would become the cornerstone of their brand. The show features the duo trying bizarre food combinations, playing ridiculous games, conducting over-the-top experiments, and engaging in deep dives into pop culture and internet phenomena. The format was refreshing, consistent, and deeply personal — and audiences responded enthusiastically.

By the mid-2010s, GMM had become one of the most-watched daily shows on YouTube, regularly racking up millions of views per episode. The channel surpassed 10 million subscribers, then 15 million, and eventually settled at over 18 million subscribers with billions of cumulative views — extraordinary numbers for a show that publishes new content virtually every weekday.

Mythical Entertainment: Building a Business

What separates Rhett and Link from many of their YouTube contemporaries is their business acumen. In 2019, they officially rebranded their operation as Mythical Entertainment, a full-scale digital media company headquartered in Los Angeles, California. The company employs a team of writers, producers, editors, and marketers and oversees a portfolio of YouTube channels including:

  • Good Mythical Morning — their flagship daily talk show
  • Good Mythical MORE — extended and bonus content for hardcore fans
  • Mythical Kitchen — a food-focused channel featuring chef Josh Scherer and culinary experiments
  • Rhett & Link — a secondary channel for sketches, music videos, and special projects
  • Ear Biscuits — a long-form podcast where the duo discusses personal stories, life philosophy, and listener questions in depth

The multi-channel strategy has been enormously effective, allowing Mythical Entertainment to capture different audience segments and maximize advertising revenue across the YouTube ecosystem.

Revenue Streams and Net Worth

Rhett and Link's combined estimated net worth is approximately $35 million, with each individual estimated at roughly $17–18 million. This figure is built on a diversified portfolio of revenue streams that extends well beyond YouTube ad revenue alone.

YouTube Ad Revenue

With multiple channels generating hundreds of millions of views per year, YouTube AdSense remains a significant income stream. Industry estimates suggest that their channels collectively generate between $3 million and $6 million annually from ad revenue alone, depending on CPM rates and video performance.

Brand Partnerships and Sponsorships

Sponsored integrations within GMM episodes are a major income driver. Over the years, Rhett and Link have partnered with major brands including Taco Bell, McDonald's, Disney, Amazon, and Old Spice, among many others. Premium sponsorship slots on high-traffic YouTube channels of their caliber can command anywhere from $50,000 to $250,000 per integration, making brand deals one of their most lucrative income categories.

Merchandise

The Mythical store sells a wide range of branded merchandise — from apparel and hats to mugs, stickers, and limited-edition collectibles. The Mythical Beast community is known for its passionate loyalty, and merchandise sales consistently contribute millions of dollars annually to the company's bottom line.

Books and Publishing

In 2017, Rhett and Link published Rhett & Link's Book of Mythicality: A Field Guide to Curiosity, Creativity, and Comedy, which debuted on the New York Times bestseller list. They have also authored children's books, further diversifying their publishing portfolio and reinforcing their family-friendly brand identity.

Podcast Revenue

Ear Biscuits, their long-running podcast, regularly ranks among the top comedy and interview podcasts in the United States. Podcast advertising and potential syndication deals contribute meaningfully to their overall income, particularly as the podcasting industry has matured into a multi-billion-dollar market.

Personal Lives

Both Rhett and Link are married with children. Rhett is married to Jessie Lane McLaughlin, and together they have two sons, Shepherd and Locke. Link is married to Christy Neal, and they have three children — Lando, Lily, and Charles. Both families have been featured occasionally in content, and the duo have spoken openly about balancing family life with the demands of producing daily content.

In 2019 and 2020, both Rhett and Link publicly shared that they had gone through processes of religious deconstruction — stepping away from the evangelical Christian faith they were raised in. Their candid discussions on Ear Biscuits about this deeply personal journey resonated with millions of listeners and demonstrated the authentic, vulnerable storytelling that has always set them apart from typical online entertainers.

Legacy and Cultural Impact

Rhett and Link occupy a unique place in the YouTube Hall of Fame — not as viral sensations who burned bright and faded, but as consistent, hardworking creators who built something genuinely lasting. Their willingness to show up every single weekday for over a decade, their investment in building a real company with real employees, and their authentic friendship that audiences can feel through the screen have made them an institution of digital media.

As YouTube continues to evolve and new platforms emerge, Mythical Entertainment has consistently adapted — exploring TikTok, expanding its podcast offerings, and developing new show formats. With a fiercely loyal fanbase, a proven business model, and one of the most genuine partnerships in entertainment, Rhett and Link's story is far from over.
