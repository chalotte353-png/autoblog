About Smosh

Smosh is one of the pioneering YouTube channels and comedy brands, founded by Ian Hecox and Anthony Padilla in 2002. Starting as a lip-sync website before transitioning to YouTube in 2005, Smosh became one of the most-subscribed channels in the platform's early history and helped define online comedy content for a generation.

The channel gained massive popularity through sketch comedy videos, parodies, and gaming content. At their peak, Smosh held the record for most-subscribed YouTube channel, a position they maintained for several years. Their success helped legitimize YouTube as a platform for professional content creation.

Business Evolution

Smosh has evolved from a two-person operation into a full media company. The brand was acquired by Defy Media, and later purchased by Rhett and Link's production company Mythical Entertainment in 2019. This acquisition brought new investment and direction to the brand while preserving its comedic identity.

The Smosh brand now encompasses multiple YouTube channels including Smosh Games, Smosh Pit, and Smosh en Español, with a combined subscriber count exceeding 50 million across all channels. The brand also produces live shows, merchandise, and branded content for major advertisers.

Legacy & Current Status

Despite changes in ownership and cast members over the years, Smosh remains one of the most recognizable brands in digital entertainment. The estimated combined net worth of $25 million reflects the brand's value including intellectual property, ongoing revenue streams, and digital assets.

Anthony Padilla departed Smosh in 2017 to pursue a solo career, while Ian Hecox continued with the brand. The channel continues to produce content regularly, maintaining a dedicated fanbase while attracting new younger audiences.
