About Lilly Singh

Who Is Lilly Singh?

Lilly Singh, born on September 26, 1988, in Scarborough, Ontario, Canada, is one of the most recognizable faces to emerge from the YouTube generation. Known online as IISuperwomanII, Singh transformed a creative outlet for managing depression into a multi-million-dollar media empire. From humble beginnings uploading comedic videos in her bedroom to hosting a primetime late-night talk show on NBC, Lilly Singh's journey is one of the most compelling success stories in modern entertainment.

Early Life and Background

Lilly Singh was born to Punjabi Indian immigrant parents who moved from India to Canada seeking a better life. Growing up in Scarborough — a suburb of Toronto — Lilly was raised in a tight-knit Sikh household where hard work, education, and cultural pride were emphasized. She has spoken openly about the dual pressures of navigating her Indian heritage while growing up in Western culture, a tension that would later become central to much of her comedic material.

Singh attended York University in Toronto, where she studied psychology. During her university years, she struggled with depression and a sense of purposelessness. It was during this difficult period, in 2010, that she turned to YouTube as a creative and emotional outlet — a decision that would change the course of her life entirely.

The Rise of IISuperwomanII

Lilly launched her YouTube channel IISuperwomanII in October 2010. Her early content featured relatable comedy sketches, often drawing on her South Asian heritage and the experiences of second-generation immigrants. Her parents, whom she affectionately portrayed in her videos with exaggerated Punjabi accents, became recurring fan favorites and helped her cultivate a deeply loyal audience.

What set Singh apart from many early YouTubers was her consistency, work ethic, and genuine emotional authenticity. She uploaded videos regularly, engaged with her fans (whom she called her "Superwoman squad" or "unicorns"), and tackled topics ranging from dating and friendships to mental health and racial identity — all wrapped in humor. Her audience grew rapidly across demographics, but she developed a particularly strong following among South Asian youth and young women globally.

By 2014 and 2015, Singh had become one of the most-subscribed YouTube creators in Canada and was making waves internationally. Her channel eventually surpassed 15 million subscribers and accumulated over 3 billion total views, placing her among YouTube's elite creators. Forbes recognized her earning power, listing her among the highest-paid YouTube stars in the world — with estimated earnings of $10.5 million in 2016 alone.

Expanding Beyond YouTube

Singh never confined herself to a single platform or medium. Recognizing the potential of her brand and the loyalty of her audience, she aggressively expanded into other entertainment verticals:

  • World Tour: In 2015, Singh embarked on A Trip to Unicorn Island, a sold-out international comedy and performance tour spanning 27 cities across 6 continents. The tour was later documented in a YouTube Premium film of the same name, released in 2016.
  • Best-Selling Author: In 2017, Singh published her first book, How to Be a Bawse: A Guide to Conquering Life. The book debuted at #1 on the New York Times Best Sellers list, cementing her status as a multi-platform influencer and thought leader.
  • Acting: Singh appeared in several films and television shows, including a role in Bad Moms (2016) and voice work in animated productions, demonstrating her ability to cross over into mainstream Hollywood.

Hosting NBC's 'A Little Late with Lilly Singh'

In 2019, Singh made history when NBC announced she would replace Carson Daly as the host of a late-night talk show. A Little Late with Lilly Singh premiered on September 16, 2019, making Singh one of the first women of South Asian descent and one of the few openly bisexual hosts to headline a major American network late-night program.

The show featured celebrity interviews, comedy sketches, and Singh's signature irreverent humor. While it received mixed critical reviews, its cultural significance was widely acknowledged. The show ran for two seasons before being cancelled in 2021, but its legacy as a barrier-breaking television moment remains intact. Singh's reported salary from NBC was estimated to be in the range of several million dollars per season, significantly contributing to her overall net worth.

Lilly Singh's Net Worth: How She Built $20 Million

Lilly Singh's estimated net worth of $20 million reflects over a decade of diversified hustle across multiple revenue streams:

  • YouTube Revenue: With billions of views, Singh's AdSense earnings over the years have been substantial, historically generating hundreds of thousands to millions of dollars annually at peak traffic.
  • Brand Deals and Sponsorships: Singh has partnered with major brands including Calvin Klein, Coca-Cola, AT&T, and Warner Bros. High-profile influencers of her caliber typically command anywhere from $100,000 to $500,000+ per sponsored campaign.
  • Television Salary: Her NBC hosting deal represented one of the most significant salary boosts in her career, contributing meaningfully to her wealth accumulation.
  • Book Royalties: How to Be a Bawse sold hundreds of thousands of copies, generating substantial royalties and speaking engagement invitations.
  • Tours and Live Shows: Ticket sales and merchandise from live events across multiple continents added another revenue layer.
  • Production and Creative Projects: Singh has continued producing content for YouTube Premium and other platforms, maintaining ownership of her intellectual property.

Personal Life and Advocacy

In 2019, Singh publicly came out as bisexual, a courageous move she made on her own terms through a heartfelt Instagram post. She has since become a visible advocate for LGBTQ+ representation, South Asian visibility in mainstream media, and mental health awareness — particularly around depression and anxiety among young people.

Singh has spoken extensively about the pressures she faced as a first-generation Canadian navigating expectations from both her family's culture and the entertainment industry. Her openness about these struggles has made her a deeply relatable figure to millions of fans worldwide.

Legacy and Influence

Lilly Singh's influence on digital media, representation, and the creator economy is difficult to overstate. She was among the first creators to demonstrate that YouTube could serve as a genuine launchpad for a mainstream entertainment career. She shattered multiple glass ceilings — as a woman of color, as a Canadian, and as a bisexual woman — in spaces where such representation had historically been absent.

Today, Singh continues to create content, advocate for causes she believes in, and explore new creative ventures. Whether she's posting comedy sketches, speaking at major events, or developing new television projects, Lilly Singh remains one of the most enduring and impactful voices of the YouTube era.
