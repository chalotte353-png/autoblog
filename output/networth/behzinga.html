📖 Full Biography

Who Is Behzinga? The Rise of Ethan Payne

Ethan Payne, better known online as Behzinga, is one of the United Kingdom's most recognisable content creators. Born in April 1995 and raised in Essex, England, Ethan grew up with a passion for gaming and online entertainment that would eventually transform his life entirely. From his early teenage years uploading FIFA and Call of Duty videos to becoming a cornerstone of one of the internet's most successful group channels, Behzinga's journey is one of genuine perseverance, personal growth, and entrepreneurial spirit.

Early Life and the Road to YouTube

Ethan's upbringing was not without its challenges. He has been open in interviews and videos about growing up in a household affected by his father's struggles with alcohol addiction, a topic he has addressed with remarkable maturity and honesty over the years. These experiences shaped much of his drive and relatability as a content creator. Despite difficult circumstances, Ethan found solace and community in video games and the emerging world of online content creation.

He began uploading videos to YouTube in the early 2010s, initially focusing on FIFA Ultimate Team content and first-person shooter games. His natural wit, self-deprecating humour, and genuine personality quickly attracted a loyal following. However, it was his connection with a group of like-minded gaming content creators that would truly launch his career into the stratosphere.

The Sidemen: A Collective That Changed UK YouTube

Behzinga is one of the seven founding members of the Sidemen, a YouTube collective that has become the undisputed kings of British online entertainment. Formed around 2013, the group — which also includes KSI, Miniminter, Zerkaa, Vikkstar123, TBJZL, and W2S — quickly gained a reputation for their chaotic, high-energy challenge videos, FIFA content, and larger-than-life personalities.

The Sidemen channel itself has amassed tens of millions of subscribers, making it one of the most-subscribed group channels on the entire platform. Their videos routinely rack up millions of views within days of upload, and their annual charity football matches have become genuine sporting events, selling out Stamford Bridge stadium and drawing TV-level broadcast attention.

  • Sidemen Sunday: Weekly uploads on the main Sidemen channel that consistently perform among the most-watched content in the UK YouTube space.
  • Sidemen Charity Match: An annual football event that has raised hundreds of thousands of pounds for charitable causes and drawn celebrity participants from across the entertainment world.
  • SDMN Clothing: The group's merchandise and apparel brand, which generates significant revenue and has expanded into a legitimate fashion label.

Solo Channel and Personal Brand

Beyond his work with the Sidemen, Ethan has cultivated a thriving solo channel under the Behzinga name. His individual content includes gaming videos, vlogs, challenges, and deeply personal documentary-style content. His solo channel has surpassed 4.5 million subscribers, a testament to his ability to connect with audiences on an individual level beyond the Sidemen's collective appeal.

One of the most defining chapters of Behzinga's solo career came in 2019 when he undertook a dramatic fitness transformation. After years of being the butt of jokes about his weight — often self-inflicted humour that he embraced — Ethan decided to make a genuine change. He documented his journey intensely, losing significant weight and training rigorously to complete the 2019 London Marathon. Not only did he finish the race, but he did so while raising over £100,000 for the charity YoungMinds, which focuses on youth mental health. The story resonated deeply with fans and non-fans alike, earning Ethan widespread respect and media attention beyond the typical YouTube sphere.

Behzinga Net Worth: Breaking Down the Numbers

Ethan Payne's estimated net worth of approximately $4 million reflects his multiple revenue streams built up over nearly a decade of content creation. While he may not command the individual net worth of some of his Sidemen colleagues like KSI, whose business ventures span boxing and music, Behzinga has built a sustainable and diversified financial portfolio.

YouTube Revenue

With over 4.5 million subscribers on his solo channel and a significant stake in the Sidemen's collective earnings, YouTube AdSense represents a major portion of his income. Channels of his size and engagement level in the UK market typically generate between $3 to $6 per 1,000 views after platform cuts, translating to annual earnings potentially in the range of $300,000 to $700,000 from YouTube alone when both channels are considered.

Brand Partnerships and Sponsorships

Like most YouTubers at his level, sponsored content and brand deals form a substantial part of Behzinga's income. He has worked with gaming-focused brands such as G Fuel and has benefited from broader Sidemen partnerships with companies like Castore. Individual sponsorship deals for creators of his profile can range from $10,000 to $50,000 per integration depending on the brand and campaign scope.

Merchandise and SDMN Clothing

As a Sidemen member, Ethan benefits from the group's significant merchandise revenue. SDMN Clothing has evolved from simple branded hoodies into a multi-product apparel line with seasonal drops that regularly sell out. The group's merchandise operations generate millions annually, with each member receiving a share of the profits.

Streaming and Live Content

Ethan has maintained an active presence on Twitch, where his streaming sessions attract thousands of concurrent viewers. Twitch revenue from subscriptions, donations, and bits adds another layer to his income, though this is generally considered a smaller portion of his overall earnings compared to his YouTube and brand deal income.

Personal Life and Public Persona

Ethan has always been one of the more openly vulnerable members of the Sidemen, willing to discuss his mental health, family struggles, and personal insecurities in ways that many creators at his level avoid. This authenticity has endeared him to millions of fans and positioned him as a relatable, grounded figure in a space often dominated by bravado.

He became a father in 2021, which he announced publicly and which brought a new dimension to his content and public image. Fatherhood appears to have further matured his approach to content creation and his priorities in life, with family themes increasingly present in his vlogging and social media presence.

Legacy and Future Outlook

Behzinga's trajectory in the digital entertainment space remains strong. With the Sidemen brand continuing to grow and evolve — expanding into ventures like their own food brand and charity initiatives — and his personal profile as a genuine, relatable creator firmly established, Ethan Payne is well-positioned to continue building his net worth and influence for years to come. His story is ultimately one of transformation: from a teenager gaming in his bedroom in Essex to a multi-millionaire content creator, fitness advocate, and proud father who has touched millions of lives along the way.
