About Valkyrae

Who Is Valkyrae? The Rise of YouTube Gaming's Queen

Rachel Hofstetter, known online as Valkyrae, is one of the most influential and decorated content creators in gaming history. Born on January 8, 1992, in Washington State, the Filipino-German American streamer rose from humble beginnings — working retail jobs and dreaming of a life in entertainment — to become one of the most recognizable faces in the gaming world. Her story is one of relentless determination, authentic personality, and strategic career decisions that transformed her from a small Twitch streamer into a co-owner of a multimillion-dollar esports empire.

Early Life and Background

Rachel grew up in a working-class household, and her path into gaming was not a straightforward one. Before streaming became her career, she worked at GameStop, which gave her both a passion for games and an intimate understanding of gaming culture. She had always harbored a dream of performing and entertaining others, and streaming offered her the perfect platform to combine her love of games with her naturally charismatic personality.

She began streaming on Twitch around 2014-2015, initially to a very small audience. The early years were a grind — inconsistent viewership, limited income, and the challenge of standing out in an increasingly crowded space. Despite these hurdles, Valkyrae kept streaming, refining her content, and slowly but surely building a loyal community of fans who were drawn to her infectious energy, humor, and genuine love of gaming.

The Breakthrough: Joining 100 Thieves

One of the most pivotal moments in Valkyrae's career came when she signed with 100 Thieves, the lifestyle brand and esports organization founded by former Call of Duty professional Matt 'Nadeshot' Haag. The organization, backed by high-profile investors including Drake, Scooter Braun, and Sequoia Capital, was quickly becoming one of the most valuable esports brands in North America.

What made Valkyrae's relationship with 100 Thieves truly unique was not just her role as a content creator — she was elevated to the position of co-owner, a title that gave her equity in the organization and a genuine stake in its future growth. This was a landmark moment not only for her career but also for the broader creator economy, signaling that top streamers had real business value beyond their subscriber counts.

The YouTube Exclusive Deal and Dominance

In 2020, Valkyrae made the significant decision to leave Twitch and sign an exclusive streaming deal with YouTube Gaming. The move was controversial among fans at the time, but it proved to be a masterstroke. YouTube's algorithm, discoverability tools, and monetization infrastructure allowed her to grow her audience more aggressively than she had on Twitch.

The timing also aligned perfectly with the global explosion of Among Us, the social deduction game that swept the internet in 2020. Valkyrae became one of the most-watched Among Us streamers in the world, regularly playing with friends and fellow creators including Sykkuno, Disguised Toast, Corpse Husband, Pokimane, and Ludwig. These collaborative streams generated massive viewership numbers and introduced her to millions of new fans.

By the end of 2020 and continuing into 2021, Valkyrae was officially named YouTube Gaming's most-watched female streamer — a title she held for two consecutive years. This achievement underscored her dominance and cemented her status as one of the platform's flagship creators.

Content Creator of the Year – The Game Awards 2021

Perhaps the crowning achievement of Valkyrae's streaming career came in December 2021, when she won the Content Creator of the Year award at The Game Awards, one of the most prestigious events in the gaming industry. The win was a recognition not just of her viewership numbers but of her broader cultural impact — her ability to build community, represent underrepresented groups in gaming, and inspire the next generation of female streamers.

The award was particularly meaningful given that The Game Awards audience is largely composed of hardcore gamers, a demographic that has historically been resistant to elevating female creators. Valkyrae's win was celebrated widely as a milestone for diversity and representation in the gaming space.

How Valkyrae Makes Money

YouTube Streaming Revenue

The majority of Valkyrae's direct income comes from her YouTube Gaming live streams, which generate revenue through pre-roll and mid-roll advertisements, Super Chats (viewer donations during live streams), and YouTube channel memberships. With millions of subscribers and consistent viewership during streams, her YouTube income alone is estimated to be in the hundreds of thousands of dollars annually.

100 Thieves Co-Ownership

Her equity stake in 100 Thieves is arguably her most valuable financial asset. The organization has been valued at over $400 million at various points, and while the exact nature and percentage of her ownership stake has not been publicly disclosed, even a small equity position represents significant paper value. Additionally, her role within the organization likely comes with a salary or partnership income on top of equity.

Brand Deals and Sponsorships

Like most top-tier streamers, Valkyrae supplements her income with brand partnerships and sponsorship deals. She has worked with gaming-focused brands like G FUEL, and other lifestyle and tech companies. These deals can range from one-off sponsored streams to longer-term ambassador agreements worth tens of thousands to hundreds of thousands of dollars per campaign.

Merchandise

Through 100 Thieves' merchandise arm, which is one of the most successful in esports, Valkyrae benefits from merchandise sales. The 100 Thieves brand regularly drops limited-edition apparel collections that sell out quickly, and content creators within the organization often have their own branded items.

Net Worth Estimate

Valkyrae's estimated net worth in 2024 is between $2 million and $4 million. This estimate accounts for her streaming income, brand deals, and salary-equivalent compensation from 100 Thieves, while excluding the speculative value of her equity stake, which could dramatically increase this figure if 100 Thieves were ever to go public or be acquired. She is widely considered one of the most financially successful female streamers in the world, alongside creators like Pokimane and Amouranth.

Personal Life and Cultural Impact

Beyond her professional achievements, Valkyrae has become an important cultural figure for Filipino-Americans and mixed-race individuals in the gaming community. She has been open about her heritage and the challenges she faced growing up, making her a relatable and aspirational figure for fans from similar backgrounds.

She has also been candid about the mental health challenges that come with being a public figure, including the pressures of maintaining a streaming schedule, dealing with online harassment, and navigating controversies — including the widely-discussed RFLCT skincare controversy in 2021, which she handled with transparency and accountability.

Valkyrae's trajectory suggests that her influence — and her net worth — will only continue to grow. As the creator economy matures and platforms continue to invest in top-tier talent, her combination of authentic content, business acumen, and genuine community connection positions her as one of the most durable figures in the space. Whether through expanded brand ventures, potential media projects, or the long-term appreciation of her 100 Thieves equity, Valkyrae's financial future looks as bright as her streaming career.
