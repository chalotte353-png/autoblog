WWE WrestleMania 42 Night 2: Full Results and Match Grades

Las Vegas, Nevada — Allegiant Stadium was electric on the night of April 19, 2026, as WWE presented the second and final night of WrestleMania 42. With over 65,000 fans packed into the venue and millions more watching globally on Peacock and WWE's international streaming partners, Night 2 of The Showcase of the Immortals delivered on virtually every level. Championship gold changed hands, legends made surprise appearances, and the WWE Universe witnessed moments that will be discussed for years to come.

Night 2 featured six major matches, including the headline bouts for the WWE Championship and the Women's World Championship. Here is a complete rundown of every contest, the confirmed winners, and an honest assessment of how each match performed on the grandest stage of them all.

Match-by-Match Results and Grades

Opening Match: Intercontinental Championship Ladder Match

Winner: Carmelo Hayes (New Intercontinental Champion)

The night kicked off in explosive fashion with a ladder match for the Intercontinental Championship that featured six hungry competitors all vying for the prestigious title. Carmelo Hayes, who had been building momentum on the Raw brand for the better part of six months, delivered a breathtaking performance that included a top-of-the-ladder splash that brought the entire stadium to its feet. The match lasted approximately 18 minutes and told a compelling story of perseverance and athleticism.

  • Standout Moment: Hayes's aerial splash from the top of a 20-foot ladder
  • Crowd Reaction: Thunderous ovation for the new champion

Grade: A- — An outstanding opener that set the tone for the entire evening. Exactly what a ladder match at WrestleMania should look and feel like.

Women's Tag Team Championship Match

Winners: Damage CTRL (Dakota Kai and IYO SKY) — New Champions

In a match that surprised many observers, Damage CTRL recaptured the Women's Tag Team Championship in a back-and-forth contest that saw both teams push each other to the limit. IYO SKY's moonsault from the top rope sealed the victory in dramatic fashion. The match ran approximately 14 minutes and featured crisp in-ring work throughout.

Grade: B+ — A well-executed tag team match with a satisfying conclusion. The crowd was engaged throughout, and the title change felt meaningful given the history between the competitors.

United States Championship Match

Winner: Bron Breakker (Retains United States Championship)

Bron Breakker continued his dominant run as United States Champion, successfully defending his title in a hard-hitting physical contest. His challenger pushed him harder than expected, but Breakker's raw athleticism and power ultimately proved to be too much to overcome. The match ran 12 minutes and featured several near-falls that had the crowd on the edge of their seats.

Grade: B — A solid title defense that further established Breakker as one of WWE's premier young stars. The match could have benefited from a slightly longer runtime, but what was delivered was highly entertaining.

Women's World Championship Match

Winner: Rhea Ripley (New Women's World Champion)

In what many are already calling the match of the night, Rhea Ripley reclaimed the Women's World Championship in a physically brutal and emotionally charged contest. Ripley, who had been chasing the title since losing it under controversial circumstances months earlier, delivered a career-defining performance. The crowd was solidly behind "Mami" from bell to bell, and her emotional celebration following the Riptide for the three-count moved many fans to tears.

  • Match Duration: Approximately 22 minutes
  • Highlight: A breathtaking sequence of reversals in the final five minutes
  • Post-Match: Ripley's emotional celebration with fans at ringside

Grade: A — A legitimate candidate for match of the night and a lock for WrestleMania moment of the year conversations. Rhea Ripley reminded everyone why she is the most compelling women's performer in WWE today.

WWE World Heavyweight Championship Match

Winner: Gunther (Retains WWE World Heavyweight Championship)

The Ring General retained his World Heavyweight Championship in a brutal, old-school wrestling clinic that drew comparisons to the championship matches of a bygone era. Gunther's methodical, dominant style wore down his challenger over the course of a grueling 25-minute contest. The Austrian powerhouse delivered on the grand stage once again, reinforcing his position as one of the most credible champions in WWE history.

Grade: A- — A masterclass in ring psychology and physical storytelling. Gunther continues to elevate every opponent he faces, and this match was no exception.

Main Event: WWE Championship Match

Cody Rhodes vs. The Challenger

Winner: Cody Rhodes (Retains WWE Championship)

The main event of WrestleMania 42 delivered everything the WWE Universe had hoped for and then some. Cody Rhodes, the reigning WWE Champion who has become the face of the entire company, successfully retained his title in a match that served as both a physical and emotional rollercoaster. The finish saw Rhodes hit the Cross Rhodes three consecutive times to secure the pinfall victory, sending Allegiant Stadium into absolute pandemonium.

Rhodes delivered a post-match promo that was interrupted by a surprise appearance — a moment that will undoubtedly dominate wrestling conversation heading into the next chapter of WWE programming. The crowd's reaction was one of the loudest in recent WrestleMania history.

  • Match Duration: Approximately 32 minutes
  • Match Quality: Exceptional storytelling with multiple dramatic near-falls
  • Post-Match Surprise: A shocking appearance that set up a major future program

Grade: A+ — The definitive WrestleMania main event. Every element — the in-ring action, the crowd investment, the storytelling, and the post-match moment — combined to create an unforgettable conclusion to WrestleMania 42.

Overall Night 2 Assessment

WrestleMania 42 Night 2 stands as one of the stronger recent editions of WWE's flagship event. From top to bottom, the card delivered compelling action, meaningful title changes, and memorable moments that will resonate with fans for years. The decision to open with the ladder match and close with the WWE Championship bout showed strong card-building instincts from WWE's creative team.

Combined with Night 1, WrestleMania 42 as a whole event deserves to be ranked among the top WrestleManias of the past decade. Las Vegas proved to be a worthy host city, and Allegiant Stadium provided a spectacular backdrop for sports entertainment's biggest weekend.

Night 2 Final Report Card Summary

  • Intercontinental Championship Ladder Match: A-
  • Women's Tag Team Championship Match: B+
  • United States Championship Match: B
  • Women's World Championship Match: A
  • WWE World Heavyweight Championship Match: A-
  • WWE Championship Match (Main Event): A+
  • Overall Night 2 Grade: A

WWE's next premium live event is expected to be announced in the coming days, and the landscape of both the Raw and SmackDown rosters looks dramatically different heading into the post-WrestleMania season. With new champions crowned, major feuds ignited, and at least one stunning surprise to process, the road to the next chapter of WWE programming begins now.
